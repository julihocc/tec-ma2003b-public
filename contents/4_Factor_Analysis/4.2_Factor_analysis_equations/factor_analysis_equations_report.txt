4.2 Factor Analysis Equations

Contents
- Mathematical formulation of the factor model: X = ΛF + ε
- Factor loadings matrix and interpretation
- Communalities and uniqueness concepts
- Maximum likelihood and principal axis factoring methods

Learning Goals
- Derive the fundamental factor analysis equations
- Calculate factor loadings and communalities
- Understand the role of unique factors in the model
- Apply different estimation methods

Practice Activities
- Implement basic factor analysis equations from scratch
- Compute factor loadings for a small dataset
- Calculate communalities and interpret their meaning
- Compare different estimation approaches

Notes
- The factor model partitions variance into common and unique components
- Factor loadings represent correlations between variables and factors
- Communality is the proportion of variance explained by common factors
- Different estimation methods may yield different factor solutions
